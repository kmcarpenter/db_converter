Tutorial

This page contains a tutorial that will walk you through converting a database and selecting certain options in order to take full advantage of DB Converter and all of its functions! Click one of the steps to go to it, or just scroll down through the tutorial!

Step 1 - Find a Database

Click the browse button to find a database that you would like converted. It will bring up an "Open File" form, which you can use to browse around your computer for the file you wish to convert.

Choose the file you want, in this case there is two databases to choose from. We are going to use the "BTE-Xref.mdb" throughout the rest of the tutorial.

When you have chosen your database, click the next button and you will proceed on to the next step.

Top

Step 2 - Conversion Options

Next, we choose the conversion options we would like DB Converter to perform for us. We are going to convert to MySQL and choose to upload the created file, and connect to MySQL database so that you can see all the possible forms in this tutorial. For more information on conversion options, check out the conversion help page.

When you have chosen the conversion options, click next again.

Top

Step 3 - Output Options

Now that we have chosen the database to convert, and what to convert it into we have to tell DB Converter where to put the created files, and what to name them. This is filled in by default, but you have the option to change these to anything you want. This can be toggled in the options, located under the edit menu.

Top

Step 4 - Conversion

This step doesn't take any action from you, DB Converter will now convert the database you selected based on the options you chose. You will see a progress screen like the one below while this operation is taking place.

If you didn't ask DB Converter to upload your files or connect to a MySQL server, your conversion is finished! Just click the finish button and your done (Go on to Step 7)! Otherwise, you'll be taken to the FTP/MySQL forms.

Top

Step 5 - Uploading your files (FTP)

This is what the FTP form looks like. If you decide against uploading your files at this point, or you are unable to configure the right options you can always get out by pressing the cancel button. The fields are rather self explanitory, but you can check out the ftp help page for more information. When you are ready, click the upload button and DB Converter will try and upload your files to the server you specified, and it's done it will tell you.

Top

Step 6 - Connecting to MySQL

This form is a lot like the FTP form. The only difference is that you have to specify the database that you would like to connect to. If you need more information check out the help page. When you are ready, click the connect button and DB Converter will attempt to connect to your MySQL server and create/modify the database that you specified. (When we say create, it means tables. The database name you specify must already be created. This can be accomplished with a tool like MySQL Admin). When every thing is finished, DB Converter will tell you so, and allow you to close the window.

Top

Step 7 - You're Finished!

You're done! DB Converter will look like it does in the picture below. Just click the restart button to start again, or close the program! Happy Converting!
